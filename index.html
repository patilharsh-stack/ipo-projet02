Main Board IPO

A Mainboard IPO (Initial Public Offering) is the process through which large, established companies raise capital by issuing shares to the public for the first time, listing them on major stock exchanges like the National Stock Exchange (NSE) or Bomba

y Stock Exchange (BSE). These IPOs are regulated by SEBI and are characterized by high liquidity, substantial issue sizes, and strict financial eligibility criteria.

    Key Features & Information

  • Company Size: Typically large, profitable companies with a high market capitalization.
  • Minimum Requirement: Post-issue paid-up capital must be at least ₹10 crore.
  • Regulation: Subject to strict SEBI regulations and vetting.
  • Liquidity: High liquidity, as shares are traded on the main board, attracting institutional investors, mutual funds, and retail investors.
  • Risk vs. Return: Generally considered lower risk compared to SME IPOs, offering steadier, long-term growth.
  • Upcoming Main Board IPOs (2026): Examples include Clean Max Enviro Energy Solutions, Fractal Analytics, and PNGS Reva Diamond Jewellery.

SME IPO

An SME IPO (Small and Medium Enterprise Initial Public Offering) allows smaller companies to raise capital by listing on specialized stock exchange platforms rather than the mainboard. In India, these companies list on BSE SME or NSE Emerge. लघु एवं मध्यम उद्यम (एसएमई) आईपीओ (IPO) के माध्यम से छोटी कंपनियां मुख्य स्टॉक एक्सचेंज के बजाय विशेष स्टॉक एक्सचेंज प्लेटफॉर्म पर सूचीबद्ध होकर पूंजी जुटा सकती हैं। भारत में, ये कंपनियां बीएसई एसएमई या एनएसई इमर्ज पर सूचीबद्ध होती हैं।

    Key Information: SME IPO (English)
  • Definition: Small and Medium Enterprises with post-issue paid-up capital up to ₹25 crore can raise funds from public investors.
  • Minimum Investment: The minimum application amount for an SME IPO is often high, typically starting at ₹1 lakh or more per lot.
  • Target Audience: Geared towards investors with a higher risk appetite compared to mainboard IPOs.
  • Key Platforms: BSE SME and NSE Emerge.
  • Regulations (New 2025 Rules): SEBI has implemented stricter rules, including a minimum post-issue capital of ₹10 crore, higher lot sizes (minimum 2 lots/approx ₹2 lakh), and mandatory 15% underwriting by merchant bankers to enhance safety.
  • Reporting: Companies report financial results half-yearly, not quarterly.
